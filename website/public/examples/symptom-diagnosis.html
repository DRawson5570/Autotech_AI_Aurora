Whining under acceleration that increases with RPM on the 3.5 EcoBoost ‚Äî combined with reduced power ‚Äî points to a few likely causes:

Most Likely: Turbo/Boost System

  1. Turbo bearing wear ‚Äî The whine that increases with RPM is classic turbo whine. At 87K miles, bearings can start going. Check for shaft play.
  2. Boost leak ‚Äî Intercooler pipes, charge air cooler connections, or BOV. Would cause whine + power loss. Very common on EcoBoost.
  3. Wastegate issue ‚Äî Stuck wastegate can cause boost problems and unusual sounds.

Also Check:

  • ‚Ä¢ Serpentine belt/tensioner ‚Äî Though usually more of a squeal than whine
  • ‚Ä¢ Power steering pump ‚Äî If whine changes with steering input

Quick Test:

Use a smoke machine on the intake/boost system. On the 3.5 EcoBoost, check the charge air cooler (CAC) connections at the throttle body and the intercooler pipe clamps. These are known failure points.

Known Issue on This Truck:

The 2015-2017 F-150 3.5 EcoBoost has a TSB for charge air cooler condensation causing issues. Also, the intercooler pipes use spring clamps that loosen over time ‚Äî many techs upgrade to worm gear clamps.

Can you check boost pressure with a scan tool? Compare commanded vs actual boost under load.
