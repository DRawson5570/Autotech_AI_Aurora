Intermittent Problems

The hardest ones to diagnose. Here's how to describe them effectively to get targeted test recommendations.

‚ö° Why Intermittents Are Tough

Intermittent problems don't set codes reliably, don't show up on command, and can have multiple root causes. The key is capturing specific conditions when the problem occurs ‚Äî temperature, load, vibration, humidity, time of day. The more patterns you can identify, the more targeted the diagnosis.

A 2017 Nissan Altima comes in with an intermittent no-start. Customer says "Sometimes it just clicks when I turn the key, but if I wait 10 minutes it starts fine." No check engine light when running.

1 Describe the Pattern

Your message

2017 Nissan Altima 2.5L, 92,000 miles Intermittent no-start issue. Here's what I've observed: When it happens: - About 2-3 times per week - More common in the morning after sitting overnight - Seems worse on cold days - Never happens when the engine is warm What it does: - Turn key to start ‚Üí single click, no crank - Lights/radio work fine (battery seems OK) - If I wait 10-15 minutes, it starts normally - No grinding, no slow crank ‚Äî just one click What I've tried: - Battery tested good (12.6V, 650 CCA) - Terminals are clean and tight - Checked starter relay ‚Äî clicks when activated Any history codes in freeze frame data that might help narrow it down?

üí° Key info provided: Frequency, conditions (cold/morning), behavior (single click vs no click vs grinding), what's been ruled out, and asking about freeze frame data.

2 AI Response

Autotech AI

Classic intermittent no-crank pattern. The key clues: single click, worse when cold, works after waiting. This points to a few specific causes:

Most Likely: Starter Solenoid

The "click then works later" pattern is textbook starter solenoid contact wear. When cold, the copper contacts inside the solenoid don't make good connection. As ambient temperature rises (or repeated attempts heat it up), it makes contact.

On the 2017 Altima 2.5L, the starter is known to fail this way around 80-100K miles.

Also Consider:

  • ‚Ä¢ Starter ground circuit ‚Äî Corroded ground strap between engine and body/frame
  • ‚Ä¢ Ignition switch contacts ‚Äî Less likely since relay clicks
  • ‚Ä¢ Neutral safety switch ‚Äî Try shifting to neutral when it won't start

Diagnostic Test (Next Time It Happens):

  1. Tap test: Have someone turn the key while you tap the starter body with a hammer. If it starts, the solenoid contacts are worn.
  2. Voltage drop test: Measure voltage at the starter "S" terminal while cranking. Should be near battery voltage. If it's low, trace the circuit back.
  3. Bypass test: Jump 12V directly to the starter solenoid S terminal. If it cranks, the issue is upstream (switch, relay, wiring).

Freeze Frame Note:

Intermittent no-cranks typically don't set DTCs or freeze frame data since the PCM doesn't power up during a no-crank. However, check for any U-codes (communication) or B-codes (body) that might indicate a wiring/ground issue.

Recommendation:

Given the mileage and symptom pattern, I'd lean toward replacing the starter. On this car, it's about a 1-hour job. Use an OE or quality reman unit ‚Äî cheap starters often have the same solenoid issue out of the box.

üî• Pro Tips for Intermittents

  • 1. Keep a log: Have the customer note date, time, weather, and conditions every time it happens. Patterns emerge.
  • 2. Check pending/history codes: Even if there's no current code, Mode $07 pending codes or Mode $0A history codes might reveal clues.
  • 3. Heat gun / freeze spray: If you suspect a temp-related issue, use a heat gun to warm suspected components or freeze spray to cool them while monitoring behavior.
  • 4. Wiggle test: With the engine running or key on, wiggle connectors and harnesses while watching PIDs or for symptom changes.
