Additional Diagnostic Context

The combined fuel trims (+33% total) confirm the computer is fighting a severe lean condition. Because the issue is present at idle (650 RPM), a vacuum leak is a primary suspect. In a speed-density or MAF-equipped system like this Honda, unmetered air entering after the sensor has the greatest impact when the throttle plate is nearly closed.

Diagnostic Reasoning:
  1. Vacuum vs. Fuel Delivery: To differentiate between a vacuum leak and a fuel delivery issue (like a weak pump), watch your fuel trims while holding the RPM at 2,500. If the trims improve (drop closer to 0%), it confirms a vacuum leak. If the trims stay high or get worse, you are likely looking at a fuel delivery or MAF issue.
  2. PCV System: On these turbo engines, check the PCV valve and its associated plumbing. A ruptured PCV diaphragm is a known source of "unmetered" air that can cause a P0171 and rough idle.
  3. Turbo Inlet/Intercooler Piping: Ensure all clamps on the intake tract and intercooler piping are tight. While these usually cause issues under boost, a leak here can still affect idle stability.
